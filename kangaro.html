Solo UX/UI Case Study

Kangaro

Rebranding Kangaro into a research-backed Amazon storefront experience.

Kangaro already sells stationery products online, but the mobile buying experience felt disconnected. At first, the idea was to create a dedicated Kangaro app. But after user research, I found that users did not want to download another stationery app. So I changed the direction and designed a Kangaro Storefront inside Amazon.

01 / Problem

Kangaro had products, but the mobile shopping flow had friction.

Kangaro sells a wide range of stationery products through its website. However, the website lacks a dedicated mobile app and does not provide a direct, smooth checkout and payment experience for mobile users. This makes the buying journey feel slower and less convenient.

Initial assumption

“Let’s create a dedicated Kangaro mobile app.”

Research reality

Users did not want another app. They preferred Amazon because they already trust it.

02 / Solution

Instead of asking users to download a new app, I brought Kangaro into a platform they already use.

01

Amazon Storefront

A dedicated Kangaro branded space inside Amazon.

02

Curated Categories

Stationery organized by school, office, craft and daily use.

03

Better Comparison

Users can compare similar products before buying.

03 / User Research

I used research to challenge the original product direction.

I conducted user interviews, listened to user problems, created a persona, mapped user feedback, tested the low-fidelity prototype with 10 users, and improved the design based on the feedback.

04 / Research Findings

What I learned from users

Users do not want to download a separate app for one stationery brand.
Amazon already has trust, payment comfort, reviews and order tracking.
Users want easier product comparison before buying stationery.
A familiar shopping flow feels safer than a new checkout system.

05 / Persona

Persona: Ellie

Ellie needs stationery for work and personal use, but she does not want to download a new app just to buy one brand’s products. She already uses Amazon and feels comfortable with its payment, delivery, reviews and return flow.

06 / Affinity Mapping

Turning feedback into design direction.

I grouped user comments into themes: trust, app fatigue, payment comfort, product discovery, comparison and convenience. These themes helped me move away from the separate-app idea and toward the Amazon storefront solution.

12 / Reflection

What this project taught me

This project taught me that UX is not always about creating a new app. A better experience can come from understanding user behavior and reducing effort. Research helped me move from a simple app idea to a more realistic and useful storefront experience.

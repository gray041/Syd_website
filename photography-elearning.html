LIGHTLAB

A product photography learning system that makes lighting visual, simple and hands-on.

LightLab Studio is an interactive e-learning website that teaches beginners how lighting changes a product photo. Learners explore Key Light, Fill Light, Back Light, Bounce Cards, shadow behavior and hands-on placement through short modules, visual examples and instant feedback.

01 / Problem

Beginners can see that a product photo looks wrong, but they do not always know why.

This project came from my own experience with design and photography. I would photograph objects and feel like something was off, but I could not always explain the issue. Over time, I realized lighting was usually the reason. Harsh shadows, flat lighting, weak depth and poor separation can make even good work look unfinished.

The learning gap

Learners need to connect lighting choices with visible results.

So I designed LightLab as a visual learning system where each module shows what changes, why it changes, and how learners can fix it.

02 / Big Idea

03 / Research Direction

The system focuses on the exact moment where creative learners get stuck.

The target learners already care about how their work looks. They may understand basic composition and visual quality, but lighting decisions are harder because the cause and effect is not always obvious.

What learners know

They can identify when a photo feels unpolished, too dark, too flat or visually messy.

What feels confusing

They struggle to know whether the issue is the main light, fill light, back light or bounce card.

What LightLab teaches

It teaches the purpose of each light, shadow behavior, depth, separation and correction.

04 / Learning Flow

From guessing to understanding.

The experience follows a clear learning path: introduce the studio mission, check prior knowledge, teach through three modules, let learners practice and measure learning with a post-test.

Selected Step

The lesson begins by framing the experience as a short studio mission. This makes the system feel less like a course and more like a guided creative activity.

06 / Learning Design

The interaction supports how learners understand a creative skill.

Principle

Immediate Feedback

Learners see the effect of a lighting choice quickly, which helps them understand cause and effect.

Principle

Segmenting

The system breaks the lesson into smaller modules so beginners are not overloaded.

Principle

Learner Control

Learners click, compare and move through the lesson at their own pace.

Principle

Coherence

The lesson focuses on lighting first instead of mixing in too many camera settings.

07 / Final Website

A calm, visual learning space for tabletop photography.

The final interface uses large type, soft white space, deep blue studio tones and yellow light accents to make the learning experience feel clear and creative.

08 / Learning Objectives

The system was built around measurable learning outcomes.

LO1

Identify

Recognize Key Light, Fill Light, Back Light and Bounce Card.

LO2

Explain

Explain what each lighting tool does in a tabletop setup.

LO3

Recognize

Recognize harsh shadow, soft shadow, flat lighting and bounce correction.

LO4

Adjust

Reduce harsh shadows while keeping product depth.

LO5

Apply

Choose the best lighting fix for a product photo problem.

09 / Reflection

What this project taught me

This project helped me understand that an e-learning system is not just a website with information. It needs structure, interaction and feedback. For a creative skill like photography, learners need to see what changes, test choices and understand why something works. LightLab Studio turned lighting from a confusing technical topic into a guided visual experience.

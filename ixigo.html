Mobile App UX Case Study

IXIGO

A smarter ticket booking flow for users who want clarity before they pay.

I redesigned an iOS travel booking experience for Ixigo as an academic UX project. The app brings train, flight, bus, Tatkal status, seat selection and payment into one cleaner flow so users do not have to jump between multiple websites and apps.

01 / Problem

Booking tickets online becomes stressful when users cannot trust what will happen next.

Users often check one platform for train availability, another for confirmation chances, another for seat choice, and then get redirected for payment. This creates confusion and makes the booking process feel risky, especially during Tatkal or urgent travel bookings.

UX ISSUE

Unclear booking confidence

Users asked: “Will my seat confirm?”, “Can I choose my seat?”, “Is payment safe?”, and “Why do I need another app?”

Persona Insight

Irene wants online booking to feel as reliable as offline booking.

Irene represents users who are transitioning from offline ticket booking to digital travel platforms. She wants speed, but not at the cost of clarity. Her biggest need is knowing whether her ticket, seat and payment are actually secure before she commits.

06 / Major Design Iterations

Each design change removed one major booking fear.

Iteration 1

Seat selection as per user will.

Instead of leaving seat preference uncertain, the app lets users choose their exact seat. This gives users more control and reduces the feeling of waiting and hoping.

Less uncertainty More control Clear seat choice

10 / Reflection

What this project taught me

This project taught me that travel booking UX is about trust, not just speed. Users want to know what will happen before they commit. Testing helped me turn a basic app flow into a more confident experience with clearer seat choice, booking status and payment decisions.
